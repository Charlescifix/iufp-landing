What does IUFP mean?

IUFP stands for International University Foundation Programme — an accelerated pre‑degree pathway meticulously designed to equip ambitious students with the academic excellence, English proficiency, and subject mastery required for direct entry into Year 1 of undergraduate programmes at distinguished UK universities.

Accreditation & Quality Assurance

IUFP is proudly recognized as an ATHE Endorsed Programme. The Awards for Training and Higher Education (ATHE) is regulated by Ofqual, the Office of Qualifications and Examinations Regulation in the United Kingdom. All successful IUFP students receive an official ATHE Certificate of Completion alongside their comprehensive IUFP assessment results.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about IUFP

Who is eligible to apply?

Students aged 16 and above who have successfully completed secondary school education with at least five credits, including English and Mathematics (or internationally recognized equivalents).

How long does the programme take?

The standard programme duration is 9–12 months. An intensive accelerated route of 6–9 months is available depending on your elective module selections and individual study pace.

Do I need IELTS certification?

IELTS requirements vary by country of origin and destination university. Many students do not require IELTS. Please consult our 'Admissions Criteria by Countries' documentation for specific requirements applicable to your situation.

Where are examinations conducted?

All IUFP examinations are administered at our accredited IUFP Partner Schools, ensuring standardized quality and proper invigilation.

What degree pathways are available?

IUFP offers 9 comprehensive progression pathways including Business & Finance, Computing & IT, Engineering, Law, Social Sciences, Medical Sciences, Life Sciences (including Pharmacy & Nursing), Media & Cultural Studies, and Linguistics & Modern English. These pathways provide access to a wide range of undergraduate degree programmes at prestigious UK universities.
